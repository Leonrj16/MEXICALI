Why is it important to change tires?

When the tread is worn down, tires lose traction during braking and won't grip the road well when driving in the rain, ice and snow. But even if there's plenty of tread left, tires should be replaced if they're too old, at least every six years.

  • 01 What are the consequences of not changing your tires?

    Worn-out tires can also develop bulges and blisters that create weak spots on their surfaces. These can increase the chances of a sudden blowout and can also lead to skidding, hydroplaning, or losing control of your car by reducing the tire's ability to grip the road.

  • 02 What is the most common tire issue?

    Tires can become damaged for a variety of reasons, and it can happen without the driver being immediately aware there's a problem. The most common types of damage are punctures, cuts, impacts, cracks, bulges and irregular wear.

  • 03Should new tires be on front or back?

    According to Tire Review, new tires should always go in the rear. The rear tires give the vehicle stability, and if they have little tread, stability is lost.
